Uh-uh-uh-uh-uh-uh-uh Football

Sorry, while trying to think of a title for this post, a favorite Goldie Hawn movie—and the song that plays while the credits roll at the end—popped into my head. Sue me.

com nick play

For most of the fantasy football season*, I found it annoying that the local airing of an Eagles game usually prevented us from seeing a game that I was actually interested in. (Unlike my husband, who had Westbrook on his roster, I had no Eagles on mine.) Luckily the fantasy football season ends before the NFL playoffs start, and the Eagles made the playoffs this year, because I got a chance to kind of become a fan. Or rather, envision myself becoming a fan someday.

As a former New Englander (I lived there—and watched football religiously—when the headline on the Boston Herald read "Wow, We're No. 28!"), I'd be rooting for the Patriots in the Super Bowl even if the Eagles had made it, but I can't help but be disappointed that they didn't.

For the record, I finished first in my league for the regular season. I finished fourth overall because Santana Moss, Travis Henry, Chad Johnson, and a few other usually-outstanding players failed to perform in the two games that constituted the playoffs.
