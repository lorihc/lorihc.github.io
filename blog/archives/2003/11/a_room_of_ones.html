A Room of One's Own

I haven't been keeping up with my blog lately, partly because I've been working, partly because I've been painting, and partly because we haven't gotten the wireless network set up in our new house yet, and he cable modem is in the basement with zillions of boxes.

crack worms usb

Al and I managed to paint the storage room (Holly Berry red) before the movers arrived; since the move, I've done the master bathroom and my office/workroom. I wanted to get the latter done as soon as possible so I could move the furniture into place and have a sanctuary, a place to retreat when the unpacking overwhelmed me.

I finished painting my study/workroom this week. It's about the color of this box, or maybe a bit greener. It was important to me to get my sanctuary finished first, so I'd have a place to retreat when he unpacking or the kitchen remodeling or the adjustment to Philly overwhelmed me. I had a room to myself in Mountain View, but this room is especially nice: it's on the top floor of the house,

Wow, I can't believe it's been almost a month since I've written in my avocado8 blog. A number of factors have conspired to keep me from blogging, not least of which is a logjam of subjects that I *could* be writing about. Funny how when you have too much to say, nothing comes out at all.

While I've been mulling over various subjects, including a list of Top 10 Things That Will Take Some Getting Used To About Philly (coming soon), I've been unpacking boxes, piling up clothes for donation (I had this idea that I had a lot of business clothes in storage, and I did—but I haven't worn them in so long that most either don't look right on me anymore, or they're hopelessly out of style), and painting.

The most important room to get painted, for me, was the room that will be my study/workroom. It was also the easiest to get done quickly, as there was no negotiating over the color: It's my room, so I could paint it whatever color I wanted.
